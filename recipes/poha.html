Poha

Poha, also known as flattened rice, has its origins in India and is believed to have originated in the state of Maharashtra. The process of making poha involves parboiling the rice, then flattening it to create the thin, flattened flakes. Historically, poha was a staple food in many Indian households due to its ease of preparation, long shelf life, and versatility. It was also a popular choice for breakfast or as a snack. The dish gained popularity not just in Maharashtra but spread to various parts of India, each region adding its own twist to the recipe. Different variations emerged based on local tastes and preferences, incorporating various spices, vegetables, nuts, and sometimes even meat. Poha's simplicity, quick cooking time, and the ability to absorb flavors easily have contributed to its widespread popularity, making it a beloved dish enjoyed across the country and beyond.

Steps to prepare

  • Rinse 1 cup of flattened rice (poha) in water and drain well.
  • Heat 2 tablespoons of oil in a pan.
  • Add ½ teaspoon of mustard seeds and let them splutter.
  • Add ½ teaspoon of cumin seeds and sauté until they crackle.
  • Add 1 finely chopped onion and sauté until translucent.
  • Throw in 1-2 chopped green chilies, a few curry leaves, and sauté for a minute.
  • Add ¼ teaspoon of turmeric powder and mix well.
  • Add the rinsed poha to the pan.
  • Season with salt to taste.
  • Sprinkle 2-3 tablespoons of water evenly over the poha.
  • Cover and let it steam for 2-3 minutes on low heat.
  • Remove the lid and gently fluff the poha with a fork.
  • Squeeze some fresh lemon juice over it and garnish with chopped coriander leaves.
  • Mix everything gently and serve hot.
